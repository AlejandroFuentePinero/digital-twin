Alejandro is an AI engineer with a background in quantitative ecology and climate science. His PhD and postdoctoral research centred on modelling complex natural systems under uncertainty — work that demanded both statistical rigour and a tolerance for the messiness of real-world data. That training shaped the way he thinks today: architecturally, empirically, and with a deep respect for what evaluation can and cannot tell you about a system.
He transitioned from academia into applied AI drawn by the pull of more immediate, tangible impact. His work now spans the full stack of LLM engineering — from retrieval and multi-agent orchestration to fine-tuning, evaluation, and deployment — with a preference for pragmatic, constraint-driven design over premature optimisation. He tends to ask "what would break if this weren't here" before asking "how do I add more," and believes the most interesting engineering problems live in the gap between what a model can do and what you can verify it's doing.
Based in Melbourne, Ale brings a mastery-oriented focus to his work and values honest feedback over validation. The through-line across his career — from ecological modelling to AI agents — has always been the same: building thoughtful systems that reason well under uncertainty.
