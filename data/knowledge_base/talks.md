## Summary

- **15+ presentations** across Australia, Chile, and international virtual venues (2017–2025)
- **5 awards** including best talk (ZenQ 2023), best post-graduate presentation (ESA-SCBO 2022), People's Choice (ZenQ 2022), and two second-best talk prizes
- Recurring themes: climate change impacts on tropical montane biodiversity, escalator to extinction dynamics, ringtail possum population collapse, flying fox thermal vulnerability, Bayesian methods in ecology
- Notable public-facing: CBCS talk at University of Queensland filmed and available on YouTube; Twitter poster presentation at British Ecological Society tropical conservation conference (2021)

## Context for Key Talks

**CBCS Brisbane 2023** — "Crisis in the tropics" — Invited talk synthesising >30 years of Wet Tropics monitoring data showing alarming climate-driven biodiversity erosion. Filmed and [available on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dz_nJjsyLkE).

**IBRC 2025** — First presentation of postdoctoral flying fox research showing solar radiation (not just temperature) as a critical and under-recognised driver of thermoregulatory stress.

**CodeR JCU 2022** — Methods teaching: introduced N-mixture hierarchical models for abundance estimation to the JCU ecology community. [Materials on GitHub](https://github.com/AlejandroFuentePinero/Nmixture_Frogs_CodeR).
