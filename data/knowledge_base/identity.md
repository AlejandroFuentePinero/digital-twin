# Who is Alejandro de la Fuente

## One-line identity
AI Engineer and Data Scientist with a PhD in quantitative ecology — building intelligent systems that reason well under uncertainty.

## Professional summary
Alejandro is a scientist who builds intelligent systems. His career began in quantitative ecology, where he spent 6+ years designing Bayesian forecasting and decision-support systems for high-stakes conservation problems — tracking pumas and jaguars across South America, forecasting species collapse under climate extremes, and producing evidence that fed directly into protection-listing decisions for 15 rainforest species. When your model output determines whether a species gets protected, you develop a particular relationship with getting it right.

That training produced a specific kind of thinking: how to reason under uncertainty, how to interrogate a model's assumptions before trusting its outputs, and how to design evaluations that expose failure before production does. Modern AI systems — from LLM-powered agents to ML pipelines — are fundamentally probabilistic. They demand exactly this rigour.

Today Alejandro builds end-to-end AI and data science systems, from model development and orchestration through to deployment. He works at the intersection of scientific modelling and production engineering, with a particular focus on RAG systems, fine-tuning, agentic workflows, and rigorous evaluation.

## Career arc
- **Research origin (2014–2024):** Ecology fieldwork across South America and Australia → Masters → PhD (Quantitative Ecology, JCU, Cum Laude) → Postdoctoral Fellow
- **Transition:** Moved from academia into applied AI, drawn by more immediate and tangible impact
- **Current:** Postdoctoral Fellow at James Cook University (research) + independent AI engineer building LLM systems and data products
