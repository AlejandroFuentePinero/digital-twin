# Biomass, Seed Production and Phenology of *Chusquea montana* after a Massive and Synchronous Flowering Event in Puyehue National Park, Chile

**Source:** https://www.scielo.cl/scielo.php?pid=S0717-92002017000300018&script=sci_arttext
**Published:** 2017 in *Bosque* (first-author, with N. Pacheco). Spanish text with English abstract.

## What it is

A first-author field study quantifying the spring 2015 mass-flowering event of the bamboo *Chusquea montana* in Puyehue National Park (Chile, Lake District) — a rare biological phenomenon where every individual in a population flowers, sets seed and dies synchronously after decades of vegetative growth. The paper produces the first species-level description of this event: above-ground biomass, seed productivity, seed viability, and phenological staging.

The work matters because (1) *Chusquea* mass-flowerings are infrequent (15–70-year cycles), short-lived, and globally underdocumented in South America; (2) the dry biomass left behind is a fire-load risk and the seed pulse drives rodent population explosions ("ratadas") with cascading ecosystem consequences; (3) prior literature covered congeners (*C. quila*, *C. culeou*) but not *C. montana* — this study fills that gap.

## Methods

Field-based mensurative study conducted in the Antillanca sector of Puyehue National Park (40°78'S, 72°22'W, 850–1,000 m a.s.l.), in a mixed evergreen–deciduous *Nothofagus* forest.

- **Above-ground biomass (October 2016)**: 8 plots of 25 m² each. Direct count of culms per plot. Average culm dimensions (diameter, height) measured. Plot-level biomass scaled to per-hectare estimates in both fresh and dry weight.
- **Seed productivity and viability**: 20 seed collection boxes deployed across the flowering area; periodic counts and germination tests over the seeding window. Daily seed-rain rates and proportion of viable seeds recorded.
- **Phenology**: repeated field observations across the spring 2015 → autumn 2016 reproductive window classifying the species into recognised stages (vegetative, flowering initiation, peak flowering, seed set, seed dispersal, senescence/die-off).

## Key results

- **Above-ground biomass**: 33.50 Mg ha⁻¹ fresh weight; 17.66 Mg ha⁻¹ dry weight.
- **Peak seed production**: 146.86 × 10⁶ ± 130.54 × 10⁶ seeds ha⁻¹.
- **Seed viability**: 87.5%.
- **Phenological pattern**: broadly similar to *C. quila* but with timing differences in seed maturation and dispersal attributable to mountain climate (annual mean 4.1°C, ~5,900 mm/yr precipitation) — i.e. the abiotic context modulates an otherwise conserved reproductive sequence.
- **Spatial caveat**: the flowering covered the full *C. montana* population in the Park except a patch buried under 2011 Puyehue volcanic ash, providing a natural exclusion that constrains generalisation.

## Why it matters

First quantitative description of the *C. montana* reproductive event in Chile. The numbers are the practical reference for fire-risk modelling (large dry-biomass deposit), rodent-pulse forecasting, and Chusquea ecology more broadly. The methodological combination — biomass plots paired with temporal seed monitoring during the same masting event — is reusable for documenting comparable reproductive events in other long-cycle bamboos.
