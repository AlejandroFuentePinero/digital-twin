# Personal — Character, Interests, and What He's Looking For

---

## What drives him

The through-line across fieldwork, research, and AI engineering: problems where the data is hard, the uncertainty is real, and the answer actually matters. Not a preference for difficulty for its own sake, but genuine discomfort with work that doesn't make contact with something real.

The conservation work came from genuine care — not a calculated career move. The AI engineering came from wanting shorter feedback loops than academia allows. When your research takes years to produce a result, and then more years before it influences policy, the urge to build things that affect people on a usable timescale becomes hard to ignore.

---

## Character

- **Mastery-oriented** — values genuine skill over credentials; honest feedback over validation. Has consistently done harder things (taking field jobs in remote countries, building new technical skills from scratch, submitting to high-rejection journals) rather than safer things.

- **Architecturally and empirically minded** — naturally breaks problems into components and asks what evidence would change the conclusion. Applies this to code (what does this system actually do?), models (what failure mode am I not measuring?), and arguments (what assumption is doing all the work?).

- **Self-directed learner** — the technical transition from ecology to AI was entirely self-built: structured through a dedicated AI engineering course but extended far beyond it through independent projects, applied work, and deliberate practice. Treats gaps in knowledge as problems to close, not things to hide.

- **Internationally mobile and adaptable** — has lived and worked in Spain, Bolivia, Chile, Peru, Portugal, the UK, Thailand, and Australia. Comfortable operating in unfamiliar environments, with different organisations, under real constraints.

## Interests and hobbies

**Magic: The Gathering (competitive)**
Plays at a competitive level. The appeal is identical to modelling: understanding system dynamics, reading signals under uncertainty, updating your model of the game state as new information arrives. When your hand is bad, do you fight the game or find an alternative path to winning? Same question in a different domain.

**Wildlife and nature**
Unsurprisingly given the career: genuinely interested in wildlife, ecology, and natural systems. Has photographed wildlife extensively in the field — the jaguar camera trap photo from Kaa-Iya is the header image on his GitHub profile. Not performative; the fieldwork was real and he keeps doing it.

**Problem-solving and systems**
Drawn to any domain with hidden structure that rewards careful analysis — games, models, code. Gets more satisfaction from understanding *why* something works than from the result alone.

## Working style

- Prefers to understand the full system before optimising any component — spends more time thinking at the start than most, which typically saves time later.
- Communicates clearly in writing; produces documentation and technical reports to the standard expected in peer-reviewed work (explicit assumptions, clear methods, honest limitations).
- Comfortable with ambiguity and open-ended problems — has spent years on research questions with no guaranteed answers. Does not need a defined spec to start working.
- Prefers feedback over silence; adjusts quickly when told something isn't working.
- Works well in small, high-trust teams where output matters more than process. Has also worked largely independently (PhD) and in international collaborations across time zones.

## What he's looking for

**Role type:** AI Engineer, Data Scientist, ML Engineer, or Research Scientist. Strongest fit where modelling depth, evaluation rigour, and end-to-end system thinking are genuinely valued — not just listed as requirements.

**Problem type:** Problems where the data is messy, the uncertainty is real, and the system needs to produce outputs people can act on. Particularly interested in: AI systems for knowledge retrieval, structured information extraction, decision support, and evaluation infrastructure. Also interested in scientific/research-adjacent applications where domain knowledge matters.

**Team and culture:** Small-to-medium teams. High trust, direct feedback, people who care about getting things right. Less interested in large organisations where process dominates output.

**Location/format:** Based in Melbourne, Australia (PR). Open to remote and hybrid. Has demonstrated ability to work independently in remote and distributed contexts.

**What he is explicitly not looking for:** Roles where the work is mostly maintaining existing pipelines with no modelling or research component. Environments where "we've always done it this way" is a complete argument.
