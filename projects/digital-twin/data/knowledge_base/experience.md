# Professional Experience

## Postdoctoral Fellow — James Cook University
**Sep 2024 – Present | Cairns, Queensland, Australia**

Leading a postdoctoral research project on endangered spectacled flying foxes. Combines field monitoring, physiological modelling, and predictive modelling to understand and forecast heat-driven mortality risk. The goal is to translate ecological and physiological evidence into a practical decision-support tool that helps conservation managers identify vulnerable roosts and prioritise interventions under increasing extreme heat.

- Analyses ~15,000 records across 28 years and 500+ locations
- Develops forecasting and risk models for conservation decision-support
- Investigates thermal environments across roost sites from the Wet Tropics to Cape Tribulation
- Supervised a student project (Olivia Bond, Johns Hopkins / SIT) on microclimate drivers of thermoregulatory behaviour

## PhD Researcher (Bayesian Spatiotemporal Modelling) — James Cook University
**Aug 2020 – Apr 2024 | Townsville / Cairns, Queensland, Australia**

Developed Bayesian hierarchical and spatiotemporal forecasting models on 30 years of monitoring data across 150+ locations to estimate population trends under climate extremes. Produced uncertainty-aware evidence that supported protection-listing decisions for 15 rainforest species. PhD awarded Cum Laude.

Key work:
- Modelled population dynamics of rainforest birds (47 species) and endemic ringtail possums under climate drivers
- Integrated biophysical, nutritional, and population models into novel mechanistic frameworks
- Produced forecasts of population viability and extinction risk used in national/international conservation listings
- 4 first-author papers in top journals (Global Change Biology ×2, Diversity & Distributions ×2, Ecography, Oecologia, Nature Climate Change)
